What Is a Jacquard Ribbon?

Unlike printed or plain-woven ribbons, jacquard ribbons are woven on specialized Jacquard looms that individually control each warp thread — allowing for complex, high-resolution patterns that are embedded in the fabric itself, not printed on the surface. The result is a ribbon with raised or recessed motifs, a rich tactile texture, and pattern durability that outlasts any surface print. Jacquard ribbons are the preferred choice for luxury branding, gourmet food packaging, prestigious event decor, and fashion trims where distinction matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the difference between Dobby and Jacquard weaving?

A: Dobby weaving can produce simple geometric patterns and stripes but cannot control individual warp threads. Jacquard weaving has full individual warp thread control, enabling complex, large-scale motifs like damasks, portraits, or multi-colored logos in a single woven pass.

Q: Can jacquard ribbons be washable or waterproof?

A: Yes. We offer polyester jacquard with water-resistant finishes, as well as synthetic yarns that withstand gentle machine washing — ideal for reusable gift bags and apparel trims.

Q: What file format do you need for custom patterns?

A: Vector files (AI, EPS) are preferred for best weaving accuracy. High-resolution raster images (1200+ DPI) can also be converted by our in-house design team.

Q: Can I choose different base colors for the same pattern?

A: Absolutely. Once a pattern is approved, we can weave it on any base color yarn (e.g., ivory, black, navy) without re-doing the pattern setup — just a new dye lot is required.

Q: What widths are standard for gourmet food packaging labels?

A: 16mm and 25mm are the most common widths for jar labels and box ribbons in the gourmet food sector. 38mm is used for gift box wrap-around ribbons with an attached jacquard tag.
