How to Read a Ribbon OEM Quotation: 12 Line Items Decoded (2026)

May 19, 2026 | Smith Ribbon China

You've sent your spec sheet to five ribbon factories and received five quotes. The prices vary wildly. How do you compare them fairly? The answer lies in understanding every line item on the quotation.

What a Standard Ribbon OEM Quotation Contains

A professional ribbon OEM quotation typically breaks down into 12 line items. Understanding each one prevents surprises and helps you negotiate better terms.

1. Raw Material Cost (per meter)

This is the base cost of the ribbon material — satin, grosgrain, organza, velvet, or specialty fabric. Material grade matters: AA-grade polyester costs more than A-grade but has better color consistency and fewer defects.

2. Printing / Dyeing Cost (per color per side)

For custom logo ribbons, each color printed adds cost. Screen printing is cheaper for large runs; digital printing costs more but handles complex, multi-color designs without setup screens.

3. Tooling / Mold Cost (one-time)

Custom woven labels, jacquard patterns, or specially extruded wired ribbon require tooling. This is usually charged once, then amortized into the unit price for subsequent orders.

4. Finishing Cost

Edges (wired, serged, heat-sealed), fold styles, UV coating, glitter application — all finishing options add per-unit cost.

5. Packaging Cost

Individual polybags, hang tags, header cards, custom boxes — if your retail packaging requires anything beyond bulk polybagging, it will appear here.

6. MOQ Surcharge

Orders below the factory's minimum may incur a small-order surcharge (typically 10–15%) to offset the inefficiency of running short production runs.

7. Sample Cost

Pre-production samples (PPS) and production samples (PS) are billed separately. A typical ribbon OEM sample costs USD 50–200 depending on complexity.

8. Tooling Amortization

For orders under the tooling amortization threshold, the factory may charge a share of tooling upfront. Always ask: "At what quantity is tooling fully amortized?"

9. Inland Freight (China)

Moving goods from the factory to the port or your 3PL warehouse in China. Usually charged per CBM (cubic meter) or per kg.

10. Customs Clearance & Documentation

Export processing, customs documentation, and inspection fees. Often grouped with freight under "FOB" or quoted separately as "DDP" if the factory handles delivery to your destination.

11. Ocean / Air Freight

The largest variable in your total cost. Ocean freight from Xiamen, China to Los Angeles typically runs USD 80–200 per CBM. Air freight is 5–8× more expensive but may be worth it for urgent reorders.

12. Import Duties & Local Delivery

Under most trade agreements, ribbon imports to the US and EU face 6–12% duties. Factor this in when comparing FOB vs. DDP quotes.

How to Compare Two Quotes Side by Side

When you receive two quotations, extract the comparable elements into a simple spreadsheet:

  • Unit price (material + printing + finishing)
  • Tooling cost
  • Sample cost
  • Packaging cost per unit
  • FOB price (factory to port)
  • Total landed cost (including freight + duties)

Red Flags in Ribbon OEM Quotations

  • Vague material specs: "Polyester satin" without grade or GSM is a sign the factory may substitute lower-quality material.
  • No tooling amortization schedule: Without this, you may overpay on repeat orders.
  • Exchange rate not locked: RMB/USD fluctuations can change your effective cost by 3–5% overnight.
  • Minimum order quantity undefined: Always confirm MOQ before comparing unit prices.
